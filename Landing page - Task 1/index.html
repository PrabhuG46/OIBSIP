Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about ShieldVPN

What is a VPN and why do I need one?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) creates a secure, encrypted connection between your device and the internet. It protects your online privacy by hiding your IP address and encrypting internet traffic. You need a VPN to:

  • Secure your data on public Wi-Fi
  • Prevent tracking by ISPs and advertisers
  • Access content that might be restricted in your location
  • Protect your privacy from snoopers and hackers

Is ShieldVPN really secure?

Yes, ShieldVPN uses military-grade 256-bit AES encryption - the same standard used by governments and security experts worldwide. We have a strict no-logs policy that has been independently audited. Our apps also include a kill switch to prevent data leaks if the VPN connection drops unexpectedly.

How many devices can I use with ShieldVPN?

The number of simultaneous connections depends on your subscription plan:

  • Monthly: 5 devices
  • Yearly: 10 devices
  • Business: 20 devices per user

You can install ShieldVPN on as many devices as you own, but can only be actively connected on the specified number simultaneously.

Will ShieldVPN slow down my internet?

VPN encryption does add some overhead, but with ShieldVPN you'll rarely notice the difference. Our network is optimized for speed with:

  • 10 Gbps+ server connections
  • Smart routing to find the fastest server
  • WireGuard protocol support for fastest speeds
  • Unlimited bandwidth

Most users actually report faster speeds in some cases due to ISP throttling being bypassed.
